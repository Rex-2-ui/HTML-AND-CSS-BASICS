The science of collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting and presenting data for the purpose of assisting in making a more effective decision is called statistics. Statistics as a subject may therefore be regarded as the study of “making sense of data’’.



There are two other meanings of statistics. One meaning refers to data collected on characteristics of items or objects as statistics. For example, motor accident statistics, which gives a record on a number of vehicles involved in motor accidents – the number of casualties, the proportion of fatal ones, etc. - in a specified period of time.



Another example is vital statistics, which consists of the number of births, deaths marriages, etc. in a community. (These days, it is also common to refer to the bust, waist and hip sizes of a person as vital statistics). Another meaning of statistics refers to a numerical description called statistic (without “s”). Some examples of statistics are the sample mean, sample standard deviation and coefficient of skewness. Qualitative variables, on the other hand, cannot be measured or counted but can only be categorized based on the characteristics they bear. They are nonnumeric and can only be described or classified according to the characteristics which they share in common.Qualitative variable are either nominal or ordinal.



Qualitative variables also referred to as categorical variables or attribute variables, specify “how many fall in each category” or “what proportion or percentage fall in each category?” Qualitative data, therefore, consist of counts or frequencies in the various categories.
